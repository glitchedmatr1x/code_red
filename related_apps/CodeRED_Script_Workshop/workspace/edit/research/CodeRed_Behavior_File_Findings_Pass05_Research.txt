AI/TR behavior files that matter most
-------------------------------------
content.rpf:
- root/content/ai/game_main.tr
  Contains rules EnableBehaviors and combat/engagement activation.

- root/content/ai/human_unalerted.tr
  Contains commented-out LAW_HOSTILE and Hostile behavior reactions:
    LawHostile(x) && CanBeLawHostileAgainst(x) -> BeLawHostileAgainst(x)
    Hostile(x) && CanBeHostileAgainst(x) -> BeHostileAgainst(x)
  This is a strong candidate to re-enable selectively for faction chaos.

- root/content/ai/animal_main.tr
  Converts damage, attacks, threats, and objective enemies into:
    DynamicEnemyOfMySpecies(x)
    StaticEnemyOfMySpecies(x)
    EnemyOfMySpecies(x)
  This is the best animal hostility hook.

- root/content/ai/animal_species_rules.tr
  Defines species behavior flags:
    AnimalBehaviorSocial
    AnimalBehaviorTame
    AnimalBehaviorShy
    AnimalBehaviorStationary
    AnimalCapabilityFighting
  Many passive/domestic species have AnimalCapabilityFighting commented out.

- root/content/ai/animal_fight.tr
  Contains actual animal attack movement/behavior:
    AnimalFightWithBroadcast(x)
    AnimalMoveAttacking(x)

- root/content/ai/animal_birds.tr
  Bird/flying behavior, mostly flee/soar logic.

tune_d11generic.rpf:
- root/tune/ai/game_main.tr
  Larger/more complete than content game_main.tr. Has Hostile/BeHostileAgainst behavior active in its hostile-attention logic.

- root/tune/ai/animals.tr
  Smaller animal behavior file. Horse rules are active:
    FiredAtMe(x) && FiredSomewhatRecently(x) -> EnemyOfHorse(x)
    DamagedByMeleeSomewhatRecently(x) -> EnemyOfHorse(x)

ASD files
---------
tune_d11generic.rpf root/tune/asd/*.xml files are animation/action-set data. They include aiming/targeting/duel-like settings but do not appear to hold the main AI decision logic. Useful for response feel and animation capability, not the primary hostility switch.
